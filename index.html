Why CS:GO Skins

Valve killed the supply. CS:GO was retired in September 2023. Legacy skins from the CS:GO era are frozen in time. No more will ever be created.
Trade-ups burn inventory daily. Players destroy 10 lower-tier skins to craft 1 higher-tier skin. This permanently removes supply from circulation every single day.
Accounts go inactive forever. Bans, lost passwords, abandoned accounts. Millions of skins are locked in accounts that will never trade again.
Demand keeps growing. CS2 brought a new wave of players. They want the same legacy skins, but there are fewer available every month.
Proven track record. CS:GO skins have outperformed most traditional assets over the past decade. Early capsules and cases have returned 10-100x.

FAQ

Why not just buy the expensive skins?
We buy everything. Lower-tier skins get consumed through trade-ups, which means even cheap skins become scarce over time. The entire supply chain matters.
What if Valve floods the market?
Valve has never re-released retired skins or capsules. Their entire economy depends on scarcity. Re-releasing items would destroy trust in the Steam marketplace.
How long is the hold?
Indefinite. This isn't a flip. The longer we hold, the more supply gets destroyed around us. Time is the edge.
Are the skins tradeable?
Yes. Every skin sits in a standard Steam inventory. Fully liquid on the Steam Community Market, third-party marketplaces, and peer-to-peer trades.
